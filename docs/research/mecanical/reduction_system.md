# Reduction System

## Objective:

Select reduction system for joint motor torque amplification.


## Reduction System Requirements:

- Compact.
- Low backlash.
- 3D printable.
- Low-cost solution for initial prototype (V1).
- Backdrivable.
- High reduction ratio.


## Reduction System Considerations:

- Cycloidal Drive
- Harmonic Drive
- Planetary Gearbox


## Cycloidal Drive

- High reduction
- Low backlash
- Low wear
- Strong, even 3D printed
- Complex
- Expensive (requires a lot of bushings/bearings and metal axles)
- Heavy
- Backdrivable


## Harmonic Drive

- High reduction
- Low backlash
- Higher possibility of wearing
- Not as strong as cycloidal if 3D printed
- Simpler than cycloidal
- Cheaper than cycloidal
- Not backdrivable


## Planetary Gearbox

- High reduction
- Higher backlash, increasing as wear increases
- Higher possibility of wearing
- Not as strong as cycloidal if 3D printed
- The simplest
- The cheapest
- Backdrivable


## Conclusion for Design

- Cycloidal drives provide the best performance in terms of backlash, strength and reduction ratio. But introduce higher complexity, difficulty of proper manufacturing and price.
- Harmonic drive is discarted for this application as it isn't backdrivable and it isn't reliable enough if 3D printed.
- Planetary gearbox is the simplest, cheapest and easiest to manufacture solution. But suffer fron higher backlash and wear, reducing precision.
- Cycloidal drives are the most suitable option for long term application, due to improved precision and durability.
- Planetary gearboxes are a good option for first tests.
